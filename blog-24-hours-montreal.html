24 Hours in Montreal

Crunched for time in Montréal? Make the most of your short stay with the one-day itinerary below.

8AM: Get your caffeine fix at Tommy

Start the day off on the right foot with a cup of coffee and light breakfast at Tommy, a hipster-meets-chic coffee shop in Vieux (Old) Montréal. Order the avocado toast topped with an egg, and soak in the gorgeous architectural detailing, quirky decor and cool clientele.

9:30AM: Head for the hills at Parc du Mont Royal

Catch an Uber (or walk, if you’re feeling extra ambitious) to the foot of Mont Royal. It's time to torch some calories with a hike up to the Chalet du Mont Royal lookout point. For the most direct route up the mountain, have your driver drop you off at the intersection of Rue Peel and Avenue des Pins. From there, you’ll follow the stairs straight to the top. If you’d prefer a more leisurely ascent, you can forgo the first few sets of steps for the gentle incline of the main hiking path. It takes 30-45 minutes to ascend. You’ll be rewarded at the top with sweeping views of downtown Montréal.

12PM: Indulge your inner gourmand with a food tour

Montreal’s reputation as a foodie paradise is no secret. But as a visitor with a limited amount of time, navigating Montreal’s food scene can be overwhelming. Luckily, there’s an easy solution to your “too many restaurants, not enough time” woes: join a guided food tour!

The Mile-End Montreal Food Tour has an expertly planned itinerary that stops at six different tasting locations (including a stop at St. Viateur’s for hot-from-the-oven, classic Montreal-style bagels). Along the way, you’ll marvel at the Mile End’s diverse cultural milieu, stunning street murals, and artsy vibes.

3PM: Fall in love with Vieux Montréal

Now, it’s time to wander the cobblestone streets of Old Montréal. If you’re a walking tour aficionado, don’t miss Guidatour’s 90-minute walking tour of Old Montreal’s eastern sector. The tour which departs at 4pm and includes a short visit to the Basilique Notre-Dame de Montreal. If you’d prefer to meander on your own, keep an eye out for these highlights:

  • Les chuchoteuses (The whisperers) , a charming bronze outdoor sculpture by Québec-native Rose-Aimee Belanger, at the corner of Rue Saint-Paul O and Rue Saint-Dizier.
  • Le caniche français et le carlin anglais (The French poodle and the English pug), a pair of bronze statues by Montréal artist Marc-Andre J. Fortier, in front of the Banque Nationale on the Place d’Armes. The artist balances a biting commentary on Montréal’s endless struggle between French and English dominance with a healthy dose of humor and irony.

7PM: Keep things jazzy with dinner at Modavie

For top-notch jazz in the heart of Vieux Montréal, look no further than Modavie . With live jazz seven nights a week (beginning at 7pm), it’s the perfect place to unwind after a long day of exploring.

In the mood to try a local specialty? Order a bottle of the Inniskillin Riesling, from one of Canada’s top wineries, to sip alongside your order of French Onion Soup. Be sure to make a reservation in advance (especially on the weekends), and request a table near the stage.

9PM - Turn back the clock with Cité Mémoire

In celebration of Montréal’s 375th anniversary, Montréal en Histoires commissioned an outdoor art installation - Cité Mémoire - that incorporates video projections at sites across Old Montréal. The projections run each night after dusk, and they're each a few minutes long. The projections tell the story of Montréal through short, snackable vignettes. They're magical, steeped in history, and a little quirky - just like Montréal itself. Click here for our tips on planning your Cité Mémoire visit.
