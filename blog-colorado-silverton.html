A snowy day on the Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad

The 21-and-over car:

The Presidential Class Car - also known as the "Cinco Animas" - is adorned with Victorian-style wood paneling, glass chandeliers, and a large outdoor viewing platform. Even better than the decor? The beverage service. The Presidential Car includes a private conductor (who doubles as a bartender) to ensure your glass is always full.

The History:

History buffs, you're in luck: the D&SNG railway is a federally-designated National Historic Landmark. The line originally opened in the 1880s to transport silver and gold ore mined from the San Juan Mountains, and began transporting tourists in 1981. Itâ€™s also one of the few places in the US where steam locomotives are still in use.

The Cascade Canyon:

The D&SNG Railroad's final stop at Cascade Canyon is a great opportunity to explore the train route. Enjoy a hot beverage by one of the fire pits or walk to the footbridge that straddles the Animas River. And make sure to get your photo taken with the locomotive during the stop at Cascade Canyon.

The Scenery:

The train climbs 3,000 feet between Durango and Silverton, making it the perfect option for those who'd like to ascend stress-free. Additionally, the tracks curve along the Animas River and hug the edge of the cliffs. With no roads in sight, this route offers peaceful views of the untouched landscape.

So, what's the verdict? Is the durango & silverton narrow gauge railroad worth it?

Whether you're a train enthusiast, photographer, or nature lover (or all of the above), the D&SNG is a hassle-free way to spend a snowy (or sunny day). After all, what's better than drinking beer in the mountains?
